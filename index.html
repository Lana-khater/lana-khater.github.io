Traditional Architecture in Virtual Reality

A walk through game of Traditional Architecture of the UAE


I chose to utilize 3ds Max to build a traditional house from scratch for my thesis project. It was an interesting assignment that gave me the chance to demonstrate my 3D modeling abilities and include components of real architecture. What began as a basic box developed into a lovely home with a terrace, stairs, tower, and a sandy texture that gave it the impression that it belonged in the desert.
Yet, I didn't stop there. I intended to provide people an immersive experience in order to further my research. I then turned to Unity and started modifying the environment close to my home. To make the experience as realistic as possible, I used a number of techniques, including player controls, tracking origin mode, gravity, and hand presence. I animated the hands to look as realistic as possible and programmed them to be able to grip and pinch in order to further increase the authenticity.
I also added rocks, sand, plants, and palm trees to the terrain using height maps, brushes, and layers. I felt the skybox I utilized contributed to the immersion and realism. A tent, cannons, hand torches, other Middle Eastern homes with the similar texture, water wells and buckets, log stacks, wooden boxes, and clay pots were some extra items I added to the project. I added an eagle to the picture unexpectedly, only to learn afterwards that the UAE has falcons, not eagles!
